How does this resource excite and engage children's learning?

Introduce your class to Adding -ed and -er to Words Ending in e with this complete lesson and follow-up activity pack. Following the four-part lesson plan - Review & Revisit, Teach, Practise and Apply - children can apply their phonic knowledge thus far to a range of new spelling rules and patterns. ER & Ed

For a regular verb, add 'ed' to turn it into a past tense verb. If a regular verb already ends in 'e', make sure it ends in 'ed'. If a verb ends in a 'y', such as 'hurry', you change it to an 'i' and then add 'ed'. This becomes 'hurried'.

Add -er to an adjective to compare two people, places, or things. Example: Flip's ears are smaller than Nell's ears. Add -est to an adjective that compares three or more people, places, or things. Example: Pip's ears are the smallest ears of all.
